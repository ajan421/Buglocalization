Bug Report: ConcurrentModificationException in ReferenceType

Title: Thread-safety issue in ReferenceType when multiple threads access weaver

Description:
Occasional ConcurrentModificationException when multiple threads trigger
aspect weaving simultaneously. This appears to be related to internal state
modification in ReferenceType without proper synchronization.

Exception:
java.util.ConcurrentModificationException
    at java.util.ArrayList$Itr.checkForComodification(ArrayList.java:901)
    at java.util.ArrayList$Itr.next(ArrayList.java:851)
    at org.aspectj.weaver.ReferenceType.findDerivativeType(ReferenceType.java:1122)
    at org.aspectj.weaver.TypeFactory.createParameterizedType(TypeFactory.java:44)
    at org.aspectj.weaver.ResolvedType.parameterize(ResolvedType.java:2479)
    at org.aspectj.weaver.bcel.BcelWorld.resolve(BcelWorld.java:567)

Environment:
- Multi-threaded application
- AspectJ load-time weaving
- High concurrency scenario (10+ threads)

Analysis:
ReferenceType maintains a list that is modified by multiple threads without
proper synchronization. The findDerivativeType method iterates over this list
while other threads may be modifying it.

Suggested Fix:
Add proper synchronization to ReferenceType's internal collections or use
concurrent collections like CopyOnWriteArrayList.

Related Classes:
- ReferenceType
- BcelWorld  
- TypeFactory
- ResolvedType
